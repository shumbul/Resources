Business Thinking for Builders

What Big Companies Actually Do

Most big companies are not doing everything. They are doing one core thing extremely well, and then expanding around it.

Company = Problem solved + Money engine
MicrosoftInfra for work
ApplePremium ecosystem
GoogleUser intent
AmazonLogistics + cloud
MetaSocial engagement

Core lens: before you admire a company, decode it. Ask: What is the one problem they dominate? Then ask: How does money repeat?

Data framing uses market-cap and business-model summaries. Snapshot context: 2025-2026. Sources: Wikipedia Big Tech, Investopedia FAANG, Investopedia market-cap list.

MS1) Microsoft - We run businesses

Enterprise infra

What people think: Windows and Office.

What they actually do: provide infrastructure rails for companies.

Core products

  • Azure cloud for compute, storage, AI
  • Microsoft 365 for productivity workflows
  • GitHub for developer ecosystem
Microsoft = operating system for companies

Money comes from: subscriptions + cloud usage based billing.

AP2) Apple - We sell premium ecosystem

Hardware + services

What people think: iPhone company.

What they actually do: sell devices and keep users in a tightly integrated ecosystem.

Core products

  • iPhone, Mac, iPad, wearables
  • App Store distribution layer
  • iCloud and media subscriptions
Apple = premium hardware + ecosystem lock-in

Money comes from: device sales + recurring services.

GO3) Google (Alphabet) - We own intent

Attention economy

What people think: search engine.

What they actually do: capture user intent and monetize it.

Core products

  • Google Search and ads auction system
  • YouTube attention platform
  • Android distribution and data flywheel
Google = intent data -> ads monetization

Money comes from: mostly advertising revenue.

AM4) Amazon - We built internet supply chain

Scale machine

What people think: shopping website.

What they actually do: run two giant businesses at once.

Core products

  • Ecommerce logistics, warehousing, delivery
  • AWS cloud infra for apps and enterprises
  • Marketplace flywheel with third party sellers
Amazon = logistics + cloud infrastructure

Money comes from: AWS high margin + retail scale.

ME5) Meta - We monetize social behavior

Engagement graph

What people think: Instagram and Facebook.

What they actually do: capture engagement and monetize behavior signals.

Core products

  • Instagram, Facebook, WhatsApp, Messenger
  • Ads platform targeting by engagement patterns
  • AR and VR bets for future platform control
Meta = attention + engagement -> ads

Money comes from: advertising tied to user behavior.

Bonus: Netflix (important but different)

NFNetflix - We sell subscription entertainment

Content engine

What they actually do: produce and distribute content globally through a subscription platform.

Netflix = content library + recurring subscription

Money comes from: monthly subscriptions and ad supported tiers.

Why this lens matters

Without this lens, big companies look random. With this lens, you can quickly decode why each one wins and where its money engine comes from.

  • Start with the core model: subscription, ads, marketplace, product, or infrastructure.
  • Identify the core asset: intent, ecosystem, logistics, social graph, or content.
  • Map the money path: where revenue starts, and what makes it scale.
